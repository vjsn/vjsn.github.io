Fujifilm X System Milestones

From this genealogy came the X-T1, Fujifilm's latest and greatest camera.

First ever X camera: Finepix X100

The Finepix X100 was Fujifilm's first camera in its eventual lineup of X cameras. It had a decently large APS-C sensor and gave nice looking images. However, its highlight is its amazing hybrid OVF/EVF combo.

First X mount camera: X-Pro 1

The X-Pro 1 was an evolution of Fujifilm's already stellar X100 camera. It took everything that made it amazing and added the ability to change lenses, crucial to professional photographers. It also made use of a new sensor technology called X-Trans that prevents color moiré while increasing percieved resolution.

First PDAF X camera: X100S

Up until now, Fujifilm's cameras had been using only Contrast-Detect autofocus, which while accurate, was very slow. The X100S added Phase-Detect autofocus as found on dSLRs for instantaneous focusing.

The Fujifilm X-T1

The Fujifilm X-T1 is the latest in Fuji's lineup of great camera after another. It combines the ergonomics of an SLR style design with the weight and size savings of a mirrorless camera. It has a PDAF focusing system and a huge .77x EVF that bests most dSLRs OVFs. Also, it has an improved version of the X-Trans sensor that was found in the X-Pro 1
